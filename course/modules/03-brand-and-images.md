# Module 03: Brand & Visual Assets
**Time: 10 minutes core | 35 minutes expanded**

---

## Why Visuals Matter

Your website is not text on a screen. It's a first impression.

Studies show people decide in milliseconds whether a site looks professional. Good images, consistent colors, and clean typography build trust instantly.

You don't need to hire a designer. You don't need expensive stock photos. You just need the right prompts and the right tool.

This module teaches you to generate professional brand images and set up visual consistency. By the end, you'll have a cohesive visual identity.

---

## Core: Generate Your Brand Images (10 minutes)

### Step 1: Run the brand-to-images Skill

The brand-to-images skill generates image prompts based on your business.

1. In Claude Code chat, type: `/brand-to-images`
2. It will ask: "Tell me about your brand"
3. Describe your business in 2–3 sentences (use your brief from Module 02)
4. Claude generates 9–12 image prompts tailored to your brand

Example output:
```
Hero Image Prompt: "Clean, modern office with diverse team collaborating around a whiteboard, bright natural light, minimalist aesthetic, no stock photo feel"

Feature Image 1: "Laptop showing analytics dashboard, warm tones, professional but approachable"

Feature Image 2: "Handshake between business professional and diverse client, warm lighting, trustworthy"

Team Member Placeholder: "Professional headshot of consultant in business casual, neutral background, confident smile"
```

These prompts are specific enough to create professional images but flexible enough to match your style.

---

### Step 2: Create a Manus Project

Manus is your design tool. You'll use it to generate images from the prompts.

1. Go to [manus.app](https://manus.app) (or ask for access)
2. Click **"Create New Project"**
3. Name it after your business
4. Upload your site-brief.json or paste your brand description
5. Click **"Create"**

You now have a Manus workspace for your brand.

---

### Step 3: Generate Images with Nano Banana

Nano Banana is the AI image generator that Manus uses. It's trained to create professional visuals.

1. In your Manus project, click **"Generate Images"**
2. Paste your image prompts from Claude Code
3. For each prompt, click **"Generate"**
4. Let it run (takes 10–30 seconds per image)
5. Review the results

You'll get 3–4 variations for each prompt. Pick the one that feels most like your brand.

Example:
- For "hero image," you might get 4 options. Maybe #3 looks right—confident, modern, not cheesy.
- For "team member," pick the one with the right vibe (friendly but professional).

---

### Step 4: Download Your Images

1. In Manus, select the images you like
2. Click **"Download"**
3. They'll save as high-resolution files (2000x1200px or larger)
4. Create a folder on your computer called "site-images"
5. Move all downloaded images there

You now have a library of professional images custom to your brand.

## Expanded: Building a Full Visual Identity (25 minutes more)

Professional brands need consistency. Color, typography, imagery style—they all matter.

### Create Brand Guidelines from Scratch

If you don't already have a brand guide, create a simple one.

Use this template:

**Logo & Wordmark**
- Primary logo (full version)
- Logo mark only (icon version)
- Minimum size and clear space

**Color Palette**
- Primary color (your main brand color)
- Secondary color (accent)
- Neutrals (grays, blacks)
- How to use each (primary for headings, secondary for CTAs, etc.)

Example:
```
Primary: #2563eb (Professional blue)
Secondary: #f59e0b (Warm accent)
Neutral 1: #1f2937 (Dark text)
Neutral 2: #f9fafb (Light background)
```

**Typography**
- Heading font (one option)
- Body font (one option)
- Sizes (H1, H2, H3, body text)

Example:
```
Headings: Inter, bold
Body: Open Sans, regular
Sizes: H1=32px, H2=24px, H3=18px, Body=16px
```

**Imagery Style**
- Photography style (professional, casual, vibrant, minimalist, etc.)
- Illustration style (if using illustrations)
- Do you use faces? Lifestyle? Abstract?

Example:
```
Photography: Bright, natural light, diverse people, real office/outdoor settings
Illustrations: Minimalist, 2-color, clean lines
Faces: Yes, to build trust and relatability
```

**Tone of Voice**
- How do you write? (Formal, friendly, technical, conversational?)
- Words you use often
- Words you avoid

Example:
```
We are: Friendly, expert, helpful
We write: Clear, conversational, jargon-free
We avoid: Corporate buzzwords, passive voice
```

Document this in a simple Google Doc or markdown file. Share it with Claude Code when generating your site. It ensures consistency across pages.

### Color Theory for Non-Designers

You don't need to know Pantone or color science. Just understand these basics:

**Primary Color:** Your main brand color. It should appear in your logo and heading text. Choose one that resonates with your audience.
- Blue = trustworthy, professional
- Green = growth, eco-friendly, health
- Orange = creative, energetic, approachable
- Red = urgent, powerful, attention-grabbing
- Purple = premium, creative, innovative

**Secondary Color:** An accent that highlights CTAs (buttons, links). It should contrast with your primary color so it pops.
- If primary is dark blue, secondary might be gold or bright orange
- If primary is green, secondary might be coral or white

**Neutrals:** Grays and blacks for text. Light grays or off-white for backgrounds.
- Dark text on light background reads best
- Avoid pure black (#000000) on white—it's harsh. Use dark gray (#1f2937) instead

**The 60/30/10 Rule:**
- 60% primary color (dominant, appears everywhere)
- 30% secondary color (accents, CTAs)
- 10% accent or tertiary (special moments)

Don't overthink it. Pick colors that make you feel like your brand. Trust your gut.

---

### Typography for Non-Designers

Fonts have personality. You're not choosing based on technical metrics—you're choosing based on feel.

**Serif vs. Sans-Serif**
- **Serif** (little feet on letters): Formal, traditional, trustworthy. Classic. Examples: Georgia, Merriweather
- **Sans-Serif** (no feet): Modern, clean, friendly. Contemporary. Examples: Helvetica, Inter, Open Sans

For most modern brands, use:
- **Headings:** A bold sans-serif (Montserrat, Poppins, Outfit)
- **Body:** A readable sans-serif (Inter, Open Sans, Rubik)

Avoid mixing too many fonts. Pick one heading font, one body font. Done.

**Font Sizes Matter**
- Headings should be large (24–48px depending on importance)
- Body text should be 16px minimum (14px is too small and hurts readability)
- Line spacing should be 1.5–1.8 (not cramped)

**Where to Find Free Fonts**
- [Google Fonts](https://fonts.google.com) – 1000+ free fonts, easy to use
- [Font Pair](https://www.fontpair.co) – Pre-matched font combos
- [DaFont](https://www.dafont.com) – Creative fonts (use carefully; some are too quirky for professional sites)

Pick fonts that match your brand personality. If you're trustworthy and analytical, pick a clean sans-serif. If you're creative and energetic, pick something with more personality.

### Building a Full Image Library

Don't just generate hero images. Build a complete library:

**Essential Images:**
- Hero image (large, above the fold)
- Feature images for each section (benefits, services, etc.)
- Team member headshots
- Icon illustrations (for callouts, features, etc.)
- Button/CTA backgrounds
- Testimonial avatars

**Optional but Powerful:**
- Process diagram (how you work)
- Before/after comparison (if applicable)
- Product/service mockups
- Video thumbnails (if you have videos)
- Social media preview images
- Blog featured images (if you have a blog)

**Generate Variations**
For each image type, generate 2–3 variations. You might find that option #2 actually works better in context than #1.

Keep all variations labeled:
- `hero-image-option-1.jpg`
- `hero-image-option-2.jpg`
- etc.

You can always swap them out later via Manus.

---

### Image Optimization & Formats

Claude Code will optimize images automatically, but understand these formats:

**JPEG** – Smaller file size, great for photos, slight quality loss
**PNG** – Larger file size, lossless, good for graphics with transparency
**WebP** – Modern format, smaller than JPEG, better quality

Claude Code will use the right format for each image. You don't need to worry.

What you do need to know: **smaller files = faster loading = better SEO = happier users.**

Avoid uploading images larger than 2MB. Manus compresses them automatically, but check after you download.

---

### Using Stock Images Strategically

Sometimes AI-generated images aren't enough. You might need:
- A specific industry photo (hospital, construction, etc.)
- A specific lifestyle (person using your product, etc.)

Free stock image sites:
- [Unsplash](https://unsplash.com) – Beautiful, no attribution required
- [Pexels](https://www.pexels.com) – High quality, free
- [Pixabay](https://pixabay.com) – Huge library, free for commercial use

Paid (often worth it for professional results):
- [Shutterstock](https://www.shutterstock.com)
- [Getty Images](https://www.gettyimages.com)

Mix AI-generated and stock images. They complement each other well.

## Callout: Don't Chase Perfection

Your first image library won't be perfect. That's fine.

Ship with 80% perfect images. Use what you have now. Update later when you find something better.

This is iterative. Nothing is permanent.

## Tip: Create a Visual Mood Board

Before you generate, spend 5 minutes on Pinterest or design inspiration sites. Save images that feel like your brand.

Not to copy them—to absorb the vibe. Then tell Manus: "I want images that feel like this mood board."

This 5-minute investment creates much better results.
