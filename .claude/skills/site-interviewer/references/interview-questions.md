# Interview Questions Bank

This is the complete question bank for the site-interviewer skill. Use these as a guide, but feel free to adapt based on the conversation's natural flow.

---

## Section 1: Business Essentials

### Core Questions

**1.1 Business Name & Overview**
"Let's start with the basics. What's your business name, and how would you describe what you do in one sentence?"

- *Listen for:* Industry, primary service/product, positioning
- *Follow-up if vague:* "Can you give me a concrete example of what a typical customer hire you for?"

**1.2 Business Stage & Mission**
"Where are you in your business journey? Are you just starting out, already established, or scaling up? And what's the mission behind what you do—why does this work matter to you?"

- *Listen for:* Startup vs. established, founder passion, long-term vision
- *Follow-up:* "What problem are you solving for people?"

**1.3 Website Purpose**
"What do you want your website to accomplish? Are we talking lead generation, selling products, establishing credibility, or something else?"

- *Listen for:* Primary goal (sales, leads, trust, information, community)
- *Follow-up:* "How would you measure success? What does 'good' look like for you?"

## Section 2: Audience & Market

### Core Questions

**2.1 Target Audience**
"Who are your ideal customers or clients? Describe them to me like you're explaining to a friend—age, job, situation, what they care about."

- *Listen for:* Demographics, psychographics, pain points
- *Follow-up:* "What's their typical challenge that brings them to you?"

**2.2 Geographic Focus**
"Is your business local, regional, national, or global? Where do most of your customers come from, or where do you want to reach?"

- *Listen for:* Local emphasis, national reach, international
- *Note:* This matters for SEO and marketing language

**2.3 Competitive Landscape**
"Are there other companies doing what you do? Who are your main competitors, and what are they doing well or poorly?"

- *Listen for:* Competitor names/websites, market saturation
- *Follow-up:* "What makes you different from them?"

**2.4 Unique Value**
"What's your competitive advantage? Why should someone choose you over the alternatives?"

- *Listen for:* Price, quality, service, innovation, relationships, specialization
- *Follow-up:* "Is that something your website should emphasize?"

## Section 3: Website Pages

### Core Questions

**3.1 Essential Pages**
"Every website needs a home page, an about page, and a way for people to contact you. Beyond those, what other pages does your site need? Think about what visitors should be able to do."

- *Listen for:* Services, portfolio, pricing, blog, resources, testimonials, FAQ
- *Follow-up:* "Why do you need that page? What will it accomplish?"

**3.2 Page Descriptions**
"Let's go through each page. For [page name], what should that page accomplish? What should it communicate?"

- *Example:* "About page should show who we are and why we're trustworthy"
- *Listen for:* Purpose, key information, calls-to-action

**3.3 Optional Features**
"Are there any pages you might add later but don't need right away? Like a blog, case studies, or a resource library?"

- *Listen for:* Future roadmap, nice-to-have features
- *Note:* Good to know for architecture

## Section 4: Brand Identity

### Core Questions

**4.1 Colors**
"Do you have brand colors, or should we establish them? If you do, what are they and why did you choose them? If not, what feeling do you want your brand to evoke?"

- *Listen for:* Hex colors or descriptions (navy, gold, lime green)
- *Follow-up:* "Does your audience expect a particular color? Like, would tech startups expect something different than law firms?"

**4.2 Fonts & Typography**
"Do you have a preference for fonts? Modern and geometric, elegant and serif, clean and minimal? Or should we figure that out together?"

- *Listen for:* Font preferences, no preference, inspiration from other brands
- *Note:* If no preference, you'll recommend based on tone

**4.3 Logo & Visual Identity**
"Do you have a logo already? If yes, do you have a file we should use? If not, do you have a vision for what it should look like, or should we develop that during design?"

- *Listen for:* Logo exists (get URL), needs to be created, logo brief
- *Follow-up:* "Does your logo need to work in black and white? Will it appear on tiny social media icons?"

**4.4 Tone & Mood**
"How should your brand 'sound' and 'feel'? Professional and trustworthy? Friendly and approachable? Bold and edgy? Pick a few words that describe the vibe."

- *Listen for:* Tone descriptors (professional, playful, casual, formal, bold, minimal, luxury, etc.)
- *Follow-up:* "How would your ideal customer describe working with you?"

**4.5 Brand Assets**
"Do you have any existing brand guidelines, style guides, or other materials we should know about?"

- *Listen for:* Brand book, style guide, swipe file, inspiration boards
- *Note:* Ask for URLs if they have them

## Section 5: Features & Functionality

### Core Questions

**5.1 Contact Form**
"Do you want a contact form on your site? If yes, what information should people fill out? Just name and email, or also phone, company, budget, etc.?"

- *Listen for:* Yes/no, form fields
- *Follow-up:* "Where should those submissions go? Should they email you directly?"

**5.2 Newsletter Signup**
"Would you like to grow an email list through the website? Should there be an easy way for people to subscribe to your newsletter?"

- *Listen for:* Yes/no, email service provider (Mailchimp, ConvertKit, etc.)
- *Follow-up if yes:* "Where should signups go?"

**5.3 Analytics**
"Do you want to track visitor behavior? Google Analytics, Fathom, or something else?"

- *Listen for:* Yes/no, preference
- *Note:* Google Analytics is free, Fathom is privacy-first alternative

**5.4 Blog**
"Is blogging part of your strategy? If yes, how often do you plan to publish—weekly, monthly, whenever?"

- *Listen for:* Yes/no, frequency
- *Follow-up if yes:* "What topics will you cover?"

**5.5 Dark Mode & Accessibility**
"Do you want a dark mode option? Any other accessibility features important to you?"

- *Listen for:* Dark mode yes/no
- *Note:* Dark mode is trendy, not essential; prioritize above other features if bandwidth is tight

**5.6 Internationalization**
"Do you need the website in multiple languages, or is English only?"

- *Listen for:* Single language, multi-language, specific languages
- *Follow-up if multi:* "Which languages are priorities?"

## Section 6: Content Strategy

### Core Questions

**6.1 Key Messages**
"What are the 3-5 most important things you want people to understand about your business when they leave your site?"

- *Listen for:* Core value propositions, differentiators, emotional benefits
- *Example:* "We're affordable, we're fast, and we care about customer success"

**6.2 Calls to Action**
"What do you want visitors to do after they learn about you? Call you? Fill out a form? Buy something? Schedule a demo?"

- *Listen for:* Primary CTA, secondary CTAs
- *Follow-up:* "What's the most valuable action for your business?"

**6.3 Testimonials & Social Proof**
"Do you have customer testimonials or case studies? What kind of social proof would help convince people to work with you?"

- *Listen for:* Yes/no, format (quotes, videos, case studies), client logos, awards, press mentions
- *Follow-up:* "How many testimonials do you have ready?"

**6.4 Content Tone**
"How should the website copy sound? Formal and professional, friendly and conversational, technical, fun, etc.?"

- *Listen for:* Tone descriptors
- *Note:* This informs copywriting

## Section 7: SEO & Search Discovery

### Core Questions

**7.1 Primary Keywords**
"If someone Googled your business, what 3-5 words would you hope they search for? Think about how your customers actually describe the problem you solve."

- *Listen for:* Keyword ideas
- *Example:* "We're a tax preparation service, so 'tax preparation near me' or 'affordable CPA'"
- *Follow-up:* "Are there location-specific keywords? Like 'tax CPA in Denver'?"

**7.2 Local SEO**
"Is your business location-important? Should the site emphasize where you're based or the areas you serve?"

- *Listen for:* Local focus yes/no, specific geographies
- *Follow-up:* "What's your service radius or geographic footprint?"

**7.3 Meta Descriptions**
"When your website appears in Google search results, the description should entice people to click. Should it be promotional ('We're the best at X') or descriptive ('Learn about how we do X')?"

- *Listen for:* Style preference
- *Note:* This is a copywriting decision

**7.4 SEO Maturity**
"How much do you know about SEO? Are you brand new to this, have some understanding, or do you have more advanced expectations?"

- *Listen for:* Knowledge level
- *Note:* Helps you set expectations about what the website can accomplish

## Section 8: Technical & Deployment

### Core Questions

**8.1 Domain**
"Do you already own a domain name (like www.yourcompany.com)? If yes, which one? If not, do you have a name in mind?"

- *Listen for:* Domain name, status (owned or needs to be registered)
- *Follow-up:* "Is the .com available, or would you go with .co, .io, something else?"

**8.2 GitHub & Hosting**
"We'll store your website code on GitHub (you don't need to understand all the details, but it helps for collaboration and updates). What should we call the repository? Something like 'acme-corp-website'?"

- *Listen for:* Repo name preference
- *Note:* Help them understand it's just a naming convention

**8.3 Hosting Preference**
"Where would you like your website hosted? Vercel, Netlify, and traditional hosting all work. Any preference, or should we recommend?"

- *Listen for:* Preference, or "recommend what's best"
- *Note:* Vercel and Netlify are free for small projects and beginner-friendly

**8.4 Technical Comfort**
"How comfortable are you with GitHub and technical tools? This helps us know how much hand-holding to provide for future updates."

- *Listen for:* Comfort level
- *Follow-up:* "Would you want to update content yourself, or should we handle all changes?"

**8.5 Email Setup**
"Where should form submissions go? What email address should receive messages from your contact form?"

- *Listen for:* Email address
- *Note:* This is usually their main business email

## Section 9: Timeline & Budget

### Core Questions

**9.1 Launch Date**
"When do you want to launch? Do you have a specific date in mind, or is it more flexible?"

- *Listen for:* Date, or "flexible", "ASAP", "within 30 days"
- *Follow-up:* "Why that date? Is there a business event or deadline?"

**9.2 Urgency**
"On a scale of 'would be nice to have' to 'critical for business,' how urgent is this project?"

- *Listen for:* Low, medium, high urgency
- *Note:* Helps prioritize features

**9.3 Budget**
"What's your budget ballpark? This helps us scope features and recommend tradeoffs. Are we talking $5k, $20k, or something else?"

- *Listen for:* Budget range or "flexible"
- *Note:* Be tactful. Budget informs scope.

---

## Closing Questions

**9.4 Final Check**
"Is there anything we haven't covered that's important for your website? Any other features, concerns, or ideas?"

- *Listen for:* Anything you missed
- *Note:* Often reveals unexpected priorities

**9.5 Next Steps Confirmation**
"Here's what I'm going to do: create a detailed brief that summarizes everything we discussed. The next step is designing your site based on this—should we plan on meeting next week to review the design?"

- *Listen for:* Availability, preferences
- *Note:* Set expectations for timeline

## Tips for All Questions

- **If they say "I don't know,"** that's fine. Offer options, but don't force an answer.
  - Example: "That's totally normal. Most B2B companies go with navy and white, or with a more vibrant primary color. Which direction feels more like you?"

- **If they contradict themselves,** gently clarify without judgment.
  - Example: "Earlier you mentioned speed was important, and now you're thinking about adding a lot of interactive features. Those can sometimes be in tension—what matters most to you?"

- **Build on their answers.**
  - If they say their audience is "busy executives," ask: "So should the website be quick and scannable, with clear next steps?"
  - If they mention competitors, ask: "What do you think they do well, and what are they missing?"

- **Take visible notes.**
  - Periodically summarize: "So if I'm hearing this right, your main goal is to generate qualified leads, and you're willing to invest in a blog for SEO. Does that sound right?"

- **Avoid jargon.**
  - Say "contact form" not "lead magnet"
  - Say "email signup" not "CTA funnel"
  - Say "search results" not "SERP ranking"

## Sample Interview Flow

Here's what a real interview might sound like:

**You:** "Hi Sarah! Thanks for making time. I'm going to ask you about your business, your customers, your brand, and the website itself. It should feel like a conversation, not an interrogation. If you don't know something, we'll figure it out together. Sound good?"

**Sarah:** "Sounds great!"

**You:** "Perfect. So let's start with the basics. What's your business called, and what do you do?"

**Sarah:** "We're called Bloom Design. We're a branding and web design agency."

**You:** "Got it. And who do you typically work with—like, what's your ideal client?"

**Sarah:** "Small businesses that are scaling. They usually have a product or service but need help communicating their value. They're usually the founders or small teams, pretty busy, and they want someone to take this off their plate."

**You:** "That's helpful. So they're entrepreneurs who know they need professional branding but don't have time to figure it out themselves. What's the main problem you solve for them?"

**Sarah:** "They come to us because they look amateur compared to their competitors. They have a good product but the branding doesn't match."

**You:** [taking notes] "Right, so it's about credibility and visibility. That's important to remember when we design your site. Now, for your own website, what do you want it to accomplish? Are you looking for leads, selling a service, or what?"

**Sarah:** "We want to attract leads. Small business owners should come to our site, see that we get what they're going through, and want to have a conversation with us."

**You:** "Got it. So it's about connection and trust, not transaction. Now, who's your competition? Who else are small businesses talking to for branding help?"

**Sarah:** "Bigger agencies in the city, freelancers on Fiverr... but we focus on personalized service and local relationships. We're not cheap, but we're more approachable than the big firms."

... [continues through each section]
