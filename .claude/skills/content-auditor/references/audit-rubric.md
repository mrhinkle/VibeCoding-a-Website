# Content Audit Scoring Rubric

This rubric guides the evaluation of website content quality across nine key dimensions. Each dimension is scored from 0-100, with detailed criteria at each level.

---

## 1. Placeholder Text Detection

Placeholder text indicates incomplete content that was supposed to be replaced before launch. This is a critical professionalism issue.

### Scoring

| Score | Criteria | Examples |
|-------|----------|----------|
| **100 - Excellent** | No placeholder text detected | All copy appears final and publication-ready |
| **75 - Good** | Minimal placeholders (1-2 instances) | One "Lorem ipsum" snippet or "TBD" in footer |
| **50 - Fair** | Some placeholders (3-5 instances) | Multiple "click here" links, "[COMPANY NAME]" tokens, "TODO: update this" comments visible |
| **25 - Poor** | Many placeholders (6+ instances) | Multiple sections with draft copy, multiple unfilled form fields |
| **0 - Critical** | Extensive placeholders throughout | Entire sections of "Lorem ipsum," multiple "[INSERT...]" tags, obvious draft copy on live pages |

### What to Look For
- "Click here" links
- "[PLACEHOLDER]", "[INSERT]", "[TODO]", "[TBD]" tokens
- Lorem ipsum text
- "123-456-7890" phone numbers
- "contact@example.com" or "info@example.com" emails
- "[Company Name]" or "[Your Name]" unfilled variables
- "To be determined" or "Coming soon" throughout site
- Form fields with example text not removed

## 2. Factual Accuracy

Claims about dates, statistics, product features, pricing, availability, and guarantees must be accurate and current.

### Scoring

| Score | Criteria | Examples |
|-------|----------|----------|
| **100 - Excellent** | All claims verified and current | Dates match actual events, statistics from authoritative sources, features match current product |
| **75 - Good** | Minor date updates needed | One reference to "current year" showing old year, minor stat slightly outdated but directionally correct |
| **50 - Fair** | Some claims unclear or potentially inaccurate | Multiple stats without sources, unclear product feature descriptions, ambiguous guarantee claims |
| **25 - Poor** | Multiple unverified or questionable claims | Old statistics presented as current, vague feature claims, unsupported promises |
| **0 - Critical** | False or misleading claims | Outdated pricing, false feature claims, misleading statistics, promises not delivered by actual product |

### What to Look For
- **Dates**: "Founded 2010" when it was 2015, "Since 1985" when it was 1995, outdated year references
- **Statistics**: Claims like "Used by 10,000+ companies" — are they current?
- **Product features**: "Includes free hosting" when it doesn't, feature descriptions that don't match reality
- **Pricing**: Published prices that don't match checkout, subscription claims
- **Guarantees**: "30-day money-back guarantee" not actually offered
- **Availability**: "Available in 50 countries" when only in 5
- **Performance claims**: "Fastest loading," "99.9% uptime" without evidence

### Fix Priority
- **Critical**: False/misleading claims (legal liability)
- **High**: Outdated statistics or dates
- **Medium**: Vague claims needing clarification

## 3. Brand Voice Consistency

Brand voice is the personality and tone of your communication. It should be consistent across all pages to build recognition and trust.

### Scoring

| Score | Criteria | Examples |
|-------|----------|----------|
| **100 - Excellent** | Perfectly consistent across all pages | Same tone, vocabulary, formality level throughout site |
| **75 - Good** | 1-2 slight variations acceptable | Homepage is slightly more casual than pricing page, but intentional |
| **50 - Fair** | 3-4 tone inconsistencies | Homepage conversational, about page formal, blog post very casual — feels disjointed |
| **25 - Poor** | Voice varies significantly | Some pages sound professional, others salesy, others casual — no clear identity |
| **0 - Critical** | No consistent brand voice | Every page has different tone/personality; reads like multiple writers with no guidelines |

### Voice Dimensions to Check
- **Formality**: Professional/formal vs. casual/conversational
- **Expertise**: Confident/authoritative vs. humble/learning-focused
- **Personality**: Corporate/neutral vs. quirky/opinionated vs. warm/friendly
- **Vocabulary**: Technical jargon vs. plain language, industry terms vs. everyday words
- **Sentence structure**: Short/punchy vs. long/complex, simple vs. sophisticated
- **Punctuation**: Formal rules vs. creative dashes and em-dashes, exclamation points used minimally vs. liberally

### Examples of Inconsistency
- Homepage: "We're here to revolutionize your workflow!" (excited, casual)
- Pricing page: "Our enterprise solutions offer superior technical performance specifications." (formal, technical)
- Blog: "yo, this feature is lit" (very casual slang)

### Fix Priority
- **High**: Voice inconsistencies that confuse users about brand identity
- **Medium**: Minor tone variations that don't significantly impact perception

## 4. Grammar and Spelling

Basic grammar and spelling errors reduce credibility and appear unprofessional. All copy should be error-free.

### Scoring

| Score | Criteria | Examples |
|-------|----------|----------|
| **100 - Excellent** | Perfect grammar and spelling | No errors detected |
| **75 - Good** | 1-2 minor typos | "teh" instead of "the" once, one comma splice |
| **50 - Fair** | 3-5 errors | Multiple typos, several grammar mistakes, one or two subject-verb disagreements |
| **25 - Poor** | 6+ errors | Frequent typos, multiple grammar issues, hard to read in places |
| **0 - Critical** | 10+ errors, impacts readability | Copy is difficult to read, appears hastily written or generated |

### Common Errors to Check
- **Spelling**: Typos, misspellings, homophone confusion (their/there/they're)
- **Grammar**: Subject-verb disagreement, tense inconsistencies, comma splices
- **Punctuation**: Missing commas in lists, incorrect apostrophes, double spaces
- **Capitalization**: Inconsistent title case, improper name capitalization
- **Word choice**: Wrong word used (affect vs. effect), awkward phrasing
- **Sentence fragments**: Incomplete sentences presented as complete

### Examples
- "Our features help your business achieve it's goals" (its vs. it's)
- "We help companies create, manage and publish content" (missing Oxford comma)
- "The team are working on improvements" (team is singular, should be "is")
- "We have over 1000 customers they love our service" (comma splice)

### Fix Priority
- **High**: Errors on critical pages (homepage, pricing, main CTA pages)
- **Medium**: Errors on less-trafficked pages
- **Typos vs. grammar**: Both matter equally for credibility

## 5. CTA Quality Assessment

Clear, compelling calls-to-action drive conversions. Weak CTAs significantly reduce conversion rates (30-50% impact).

### Scoring

| Score | Criteria | Examples |
|-------|----------|----------|
| **100 - Excellent** | All CTAs clear, compelling, strategically placed | Specific action buttons ("Start Free Trial"), persuasive copy, prominent placement |
| **75 - Good** | CTAs present and clear with minor improvements | "Sign Up" is clear but could be more compelling, good placement |
| **50 - Fair** | Some CTAs weak or missing key pages | "Submit" is generic, some pages lack CTAs, placement could be better |
| **25 - Poor** | Most CTAs unclear or poorly worded | "Click here" links, vague button text, CTAs buried in navigation |
| **0 - Critical** | Missing CTAs or broken links | No clear CTAs on key pages, CTA buttons don't work, completely unclear what to do next |

### CTA Quality Dimensions

**Clarity**: Is the action obvious?
- Poor: "Click here", "Submit", "Go"
- Good: "Start Free Trial", "Download Guide", "Request Demo", "Get Started"

**Persuasiveness**: Does it create urgency or excitement?
- Weak: "Sign Up" (transactional, no motivation)
- Strong: "Start Your Free Trial Today", "Claim Your 50% Discount", "Get Instant Access"

**Placement**: Is it visible without scrolling?
- Poor: CTA at bottom of long page, buried in menu
- Good: Visible in hero section, repeated throughout page (header, middle, footer)

**Frequency**: Does key pages have multiple CTAs?
- Poor: One CTA on 2000-word page
- Good: CTA in hero, mid-page, and footer; multiple conversion options

**Consistency**: Do all CTAs match your conversion goals?
- Poor: Email signup CTA on one page, phone call on another, no pattern
- Good: All CTAs lead to consistent conversion point

### Examples of Weak CTAs
- "Click here to learn more" (vague, overused phrase)
- "Submit" (what are you submitting?)
- "Next" (no indication of where it goes)
- "→" (icon-only, unclear action)
- Small gray button at bottom of page (invisible, uncompelling)

### Examples of Strong CTAs
- "Start Your Free 14-Day Trial"
- "Get Your Personalized Quote"
- "Download the Full Case Study"
- "Schedule Your Consultation"
- Large, colored button in hero section and footer

### Fix Priority
- **Critical**: Missing CTAs on homepage, pricing page, or main conversion pages
- **High**: Weak or unclear CTAs on high-traffic pages
- **Medium**: Generic wording that could be more compelling

## 6. Consistent Messaging

Messaging contradictions create confusion and reduce trust. Key messages should align across all pages.

### Scoring

| Score | Criteria | Examples |
|-------|----------|----------|
| **100 - Excellent** | No contradictions in messaging | All pages reinforce same core value proposition |
| **75 - Good** | 1 minor contradiction | Slight wording difference that doesn't meaningfully conflict |
| **50 - Fair** | 2-3 contradictions | Some conflicting claims across pages, inconsistent positioning |
| **25 - Poor** | 4+ contradictions | Multiple conflicting messages, user confused about core offer |
| **0 - Critical** | Major contradictory claims | Homepage says "cheapest option," pricing page says "premium quality," appears dishonest |

### What to Check

**Core value proposition**: Does every page communicate the same primary benefit?
- Page 1: "We help businesses save time"
- Page 2: "We provide premium features"
- Page 3: "We're the most affordable option"
- Contradiction: Three different value props

**Unique selling proposition**: Are your differentiators consistent?
- Homepage: "Only solution with AI-powered optimization"
- Pricing: "Similar to other solutions"
- Contradiction: Unique claim contradicted on pricing page

**Audience claims**: Is the target audience consistent?
- Homepage: "For solopreneurs and small teams"
- Pricing: "Enterprise-grade solution for Fortune 500 companies"
- Contradiction: Conflicting audience definition

**Feature descriptions**: Do features have consistent descriptions?
- Feature on one page: "Generates reports in 30 seconds"
- Same feature elsewhere: "Takes about 5 minutes"
- Contradiction: Conflicting claims about same feature

**Pricing claims**: Do pricing messages align?
- Homepage: "Most affordable option in market"
- Pricing page: "Premium pricing for premium quality"
- Contradiction: Conflicting pricing positioning

### Examples of Contradictions
- "Built for beginners" (homepage) vs. "Advanced solution for experts" (features page)
- "No setup required" (homepage) vs. "30-minute setup included" (docs)
- "Works with all tools" (marketing) vs. "Integrates with 5 major platforms" (integrations page)
- "24/7 support" (homepage) vs. "Support hours 9am-5pm EST" (contact page)

### Fix Priority
- **Critical**: Major contradictions in core offer or claims
- **High**: Conflicting pricing or feature descriptions
- **Medium**: Minor wording inconsistencies

## 7. Contact Information Consistency

Contact information must be complete, accurate, and consistent across the entire site. Inconsistencies create support friction and lost customers.

### Scoring

| Score | Criteria | Examples |
|-------|----------|----------|
| **100 - Excellent** | Complete, consistent, current info across all pages | Phone, email, address all match everywhere, all current |
| **75 - Good** | Minor variations acceptable | Same phone on 90% of pages, one page missing email |
| **50 - Fair** | Some outdated or inconsistent info | Different phone numbers on different pages, outdated address |
| **25 - Poor** | Multiple discrepancies | Phone numbers don't match, old address on some pages, mixed formats |
| **0 - Critical** | Critical contact info missing or broken | No contact information available, broken contact form, unreachable |

### What to Check

**Consistency across pages**:
- Does every page with contact info show the same phone number?
- Is email address identical on all pages?
- Is physical address consistent (if applicable)?

**Currency**:
- Are phone numbers currently active?
- Is email monitored and responded to?
- Is physical address accurate and current?
- Are office hours current?

**Completeness**:
- Does contact page include: phone, email, address, hours, contact form?
- Are all pages missing critical contact info?
- Is contact info visible on every page that needs it (not just contact page)?

**Format consistency**:
- Phone: (555) 123-4567 vs. 555-123-4567 vs. 5551234567
- Email: support@example.com vs. Support@example.com
- Address: 123 Main St, City, State 12345 vs. 123 Main Street...

### Examples of Issues
- Homepage shows "(555) 123-4567" but contact page shows "555-123-4567"
- About page lists old office address
- Contact form goes to different email than listed phone
- Social media profiles show different business hours than website
- Phone number on homepage doesn't match phone number in footer

### Fix Priority
- **Critical**: Contact information completely missing or non-functional
- **High**: Inconsistent phone/email across pages (customer can't reach you)
- **Medium**: Format inconsistencies or outdated office hours

## 8. Content Completeness

Pages should have all necessary sections. Missing sections create gaps that hurt conversion and user trust.

### Scoring

| Score | Criteria | Examples |
|-------|----------|----------|
| **100 - Excellent** | All critical sections present on all pages | About, contact, privacy, terms, etc. all present |
| **75 - Good** | 1 minor section missing | Privacy policy missing but others present |
| **50 - Fair** | 2-3 sections missing | No privacy policy or terms, missing about page |
| **25 - Poor** | 4+ critical sections missing | No about, no contact, no privacy, appears incomplete |
| **0 - Critical** | Multiple critical sections completely missing | No contact info, no privacy policy, site appears abandoned |

### Critical Sections by Page Type

**Every website needs**:
- [ ] About page (who you are, why you exist)
- [ ] Contact page or form (how to reach you)
- [ ] Privacy policy (legal requirement)
- [ ] Terms of service (legal requirement)

**Service/Product sites should have**:
- [ ] Features or services page (what you offer)
- [ ] Pricing page (how much it costs)
- [ ] FAQ page (answer common questions)
- [ ] Blog or resources (establish expertise)

**E-commerce sites should have**:
- [ ] Product/category pages (what you sell)
- [ ] Pricing (product costs, shipping)
- [ ] Product photos and descriptions
- [ ] Return/refund policy
- [ ] Shipping information
- [ ] FAQ or help section

**Service provider sites should have**:
- [ ] Service description (what you do)
- [ ] Pricing or rate information
- [ ] Portfolio or case studies
- [ ] Client testimonials
- [ ] Booking or consultation info

### Pages to Audit for Completeness
1. **Homepage**: Should introduce site and have clear navigation
2. **About page**: Should establish credibility and story
3. **Contact page**: Should provide multiple contact options
4. **Product/service pages**: Should fully describe offering
5. **Pricing page**: Should clearly show costs (if applicable)
6. **Footer**: Should have legal links and secondary navigation

### Examples of Missing Sections
- No About page (users don't know who you are)
- No Contact page (users can't reach you)
- No Privacy Policy (legal liability, appears untrustworthy)
- Product pages missing descriptions or images
- Services page with no pricing (users can't understand value)

### Fix Priority
- **Critical**: Missing legal pages (privacy, terms) or contact info
- **High**: Missing key business pages (about, services, pricing)
- **Medium**: Missing supplementary content (FAQ, blog)

## 9. Broken Promises

Misalignment between claims made on the website and actual delivered service is a trust killer. Every promise should match reality.

### Scoring

| Score | Criteria | Examples |
|-------|----------|----------|
| **100 - Excellent** | No discrepancies between claims and reality | Every promise delivered exactly as described |
| **75 - Good** | 1 minor misalignment | Minor feature works slightly differently than described |
| **50 - Fair** | 2-3 misalignments | Some features work as described, others don't match promises |
| **25 - Poor** | 4+ broken promises | Multiple features don't work as advertised, customer confused |
| **0 - Critical** | Major contradiction between claims and delivery | Core promise completely unmet, appears fraudulent |

### What to Check

**Feature claims vs. reality**:
- Claim: "Real-time collaboration" - Does it actually update in real-time?
- Claim: "Works offline" - Does it actually work without internet?
- Claim: "One-click integration" - Does it actually integrate with one click?

**Speed/performance claims vs. reality**:
- Claim: "Lightning fast" - How fast is it really? (test it)
- Claim: "Instant processing" - Does it actually process instantly?
- Claim: "Load in milliseconds" - Does it actually?

**Support/service claims vs. reality**:
- Claim: "24/7 customer support" - Is support actually available 24/7?
- Claim: "Same-day responses" - Do you actually respond same-day?
- Claim: "Dedicated account manager" - Does every customer get one?

**Guarantee claims vs. reality**:
- Claim: "30-day money-back guarantee" - Is it actually no-questions-asked?
- Claim: "Satisfaction guaranteed" - What's the actual process?
- Claim: "Risk-free trial" - Are there any hidden terms?

**Availability claims vs. reality**:
- Claim: "Available in 50 countries" - Is it really available everywhere or limited?
- Claim: "Works on all devices" - Does it actually work on iPhone, Android, desktop?
- Claim: "No credit card required" - Is that actually true for trial?

### Examples of Broken Promises
- **Claim**: "Setup in under 5 minutes"
  **Reality**: Actual setup takes 30 minutes with configuration
  **Impact**: Customer disappointed, reviews suffer

- **Claim**: "Integrates with 500+ apps"
  **Reality**: Only integrates with 50 apps; other integrations are manual
  **Impact**: Customer feels misled, cancels subscription

- **Claim**: "Guaranteed uptime 99.9%"
  **Reality**: Actual uptime is 98.5%
  **Impact**: Contract violation, legal issues

- **Claim**: "Best customer support in the industry"
  **Reality**: Average response time 48 hours, limited support hours
  **Impact**: Reputational damage, negative reviews

### Fix Priority
- **Critical**: Major broken promises affecting core product/service
- **High**: Broken guarantees or support promises
- **Medium**: Feature descriptions that need clarification

---

## Using This Rubric

### Step 1: Score Each Dimension
For each of the 9 dimensions, assign a score from 0-100 based on the criteria above.

### Step 2: Calculate Category Averages
If auditing multiple pages, average scores across pages for each dimension.

### Step 3: Identify Critical Issues
Any dimension scoring 0-25 is critical and should be fixed immediately.

### Step 4: Prioritize Fixes
High-priority issues (score 25-50) should be fixed within 1-2 weeks.
Medium-priority issues (score 50-75) should be fixed when capacity allows.
Excellent areas (75-100) need no fixes.

### Step 5: Benchmark & Track
Track scores over time. Goal is to move from 50+ to 75+ on all dimensions before launch, and maintain 80+ post-launch.
