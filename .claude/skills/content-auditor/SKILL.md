# Content Auditor Skill

## Skill Summary

The Content Auditor reviews all website copy for quality, consistency, and completeness. It identifies placeholder text, validates factual accuracy, checks brand voice consistency, verifies grammar and spelling, assesses CTAs, detects missing content sections, validates contact information, and identifies broken promises or contradictions.

**Business Value**: Content quality directly impacts conversion rates, trust, SEO rankings, and user experience. This skill ensures every page meets professional standards before launch.

---

## What This Skill Does

The Content Auditor performs comprehensive copy auditing across your entire website, checking:

1. **Placeholder Text Detection** - Identifies draft or template text that should have been replaced
2. **Factual Accuracy Checks** - Flags claims that may need verification (dates, statistics, product features)
3. **Brand Voice Consistency** - Ensures tone and language align across all pages
4. **Grammar and Spelling** - Catches typos and grammatical errors
5. **CTA Quality Assessment** - Evaluates call-to-action clarity, persuasiveness, and placement
6. **Consistent Messaging** - Identifies contradictions in messaging across pages
7. **Contact Info Consistency** - Verifies phone numbers, emails, addresses match across site
8. **Missing Content Sections** - Identifies pages missing critical sections (about, contact, privacy)
9. **Broken Promises** - Detects misalignment between claims and actual content

---

## Why This Matters (Business Impact)

**Trust & Credibility**: Placeholder text or typos immediately signal an unprofessional, incomplete site
- Poor copy quality costs customers. Studies show 59% of users leave poorly written sites
- Grammar/spelling errors reduce trust perception by 71%

**Conversion Optimization**: Clear, consistent CTAs drive action
- Weak or missing CTAs eliminate 30-50% of potential conversions
- Inconsistent messaging confuses users and increases bounce rates

**Legal & Compliance**: Accurate contact info and consistent claims protect you legally
- Outdated contact information creates support friction
- Broken promises (on website vs. actual service) lead to customer complaints and chargebacks

**SEO Impact**: Quality, original content ranks better
- Placeholder text damages rankings and wastes crawl budget
- Factual errors or contradictions confuse search engines

**Brand Perception**: Consistent voice reinforces brand identity
- Inconsistent tone appears unprofessional and chaotic
- Weak CTAs undermine conversion goals

## Audit Rubric

The Content Auditor uses a detailed scoring rubric that evaluates:

| Category | Excellent (100) | Good (75) | Fair (50) | Poor (25) | Critical (0) |
|----------|-----------------|-----------|-----------|-----------|------------|
| **Placeholder Text** | No placeholders | Minimal (1-2) | Some (3-5) | Many (6+) | Extensive placeholders detected |
| **Factual Accuracy** | All claims verified and current | Minor date updates needed | Some claims unclear | Multiple unverified claims | False or misleading claims |
| **Brand Voice** | Perfectly consistent across all pages | 1-2 slight variations | 3-4 tone inconsistencies | Voice varies significantly | No consistent brand voice |
| **Grammar/Spelling** | Perfect | 1-2 typos | 3-5 errors | 6+ errors | 10+ errors, hard to read |
| **CTA Quality** | Clear, compelling, properly placed | Good with minor improvements | Some CTAs weak | Most CTAs unclear | Missing or broken CTAs |
| **Consistent Messaging** | No contradictions | 1 minor contradiction | 2-3 contradictions | 4+ contradictions | Major contradictory claims |
| **Contact Info** | Complete, consistent, current | Minor variations | Some outdated info | Multiple discrepancies | Critical contact info missing |
| **Content Completeness** | All sections present | 1 minor section missing | 2-3 sections missing | 4+ sections missing | Multiple critical sections missing |
| **Broken Promises** | No discrepancies between claims and reality | 1 minor misalignment | 2-3 misalignments | 4+ broken promises | Major contradiction between claims and delivery |

See `references/audit-rubric.md` for detailed scoring criteria and examples.

## Optimization Tips

### For Best Results
1. **Run before launch** - Catch issues while you can still fix them easily
2. **Run quarterly** - Content evolves; maintain quality over time
3. **Run after major updates** - Check new/modified content immediately
4. **Combine with SEO audit** - SEO and content quality work together

### Understanding Results
- **Critical issues** block launch - fix immediately
- **High priority** issues affect conversions - fix within 1-2 weeks
- **Medium priority** issues improve polish - fix when you have capacity
- Not all suggestions are mandatory - prioritize by business impact

### Making Fixes Efficient
1. Group issues by page for batch editing
2. Establish brand voice guidelines to prevent future issues
3. Create content templates to reduce placeholder problems
4. Schedule monthly content reviews to catch issues early

## FAQ

**Q: Will this audit catch every typo?**
A: The audit catches obvious spelling and grammar errors, but relies on your review for context-specific issues. Consider professional proofreading for critical pages.

**Q: How do I know what's a "broken promise"?**
A: The auditor flags discrepancies between claims and actual content. Example: "24/7 support" claimed but contact form says "replies in 24 hours."

**Q: Can I audit just specific pages?**
A: Yes! You can specify which pages to audit, or we'll audit the entire site and prioritize results by page importance.

**Q: How often should I run this audit?**
A: Run before launch, then quarterly or after major content updates. High-traffic sites benefit from monthly audits.

**Q: What if I have multiple brand voices (for different audiences)?**
A: Document these in your brand guidelines so the auditor knows they're intentional. It will still flag inconsistencies within each voice/audience segment.

**Q: How do I fix the issues found?**
A: The audit provides specific recommendations for each issue. For content rewrites, consult with your copywriter or marketing team.

**Q: Does this check for plagiarism or AI-generated content?**
A: This audit focuses on quality, clarity, and consistency. For plagiarism detection, use a dedicated tool like Copyscape or Turnitin.
